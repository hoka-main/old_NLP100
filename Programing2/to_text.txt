State, territorial, tribal, and local governments have responded to the COVID-19 pandemic in the United States with various declarations of emergency, closure of schools and public meeting places, lockdowns, and other restrictions intended to slow the progression of the virus.

Multiple groups of states have formed compacts in an attempt to coordinate some of their responses; on the West coast: California, Oregon, and Washington state; in the Northeast: Massachusetts, New York, New Jersey, Connecticut, Delaware, Pennsylvania, and Rhode Island; and in the Midwest: Michigan, Ohio, Wisconsin, Minnesota, Illinois, Indiana, and Kentucky.[1][2][3]

Alabama

COVID-19 Cases by counties of Alabama by March 22
Main article: COVID-19 pandemic in Alabama
On March 12, all K-12 public and private schools were ordered to be closed from March 18 until April 6.[4] On March 26, governor Kay Ivey announced that all K-12 schools would be closed for the rest of the school year, ordering that beginning April 6, all local school systems should adopt "alternative methods of instruction" such as online courses.[103]

On March 24, Birmingham issued a stay-at-home order (as a 24-hour curfew) effective March 24 to April 3.[104]

On March 25, Tuscaloosa city mayor Walt Maddox issued a city-wide curfew (the second in the state), lasting from 10:00 p.m. until 5:00 a.m. each day, effective March 27 to April 3.[105] The next day Maddox extended the curfew to 24 hours, beginning March 29 at 10:00 p.m., and lasting an additional week. The Tuscaloosa stay-at-home order came after Alabama Attorney General Steve Marshall's opinion published on the same day that provided cities and counties with more authority to combat the pandemic, coupled with governor Kay Ivey's promise not to object to local communities' containment efforts.[106]

On March 27, all "non-essential businesses" in the state were ordered to be closed until April 17, including barbershops, furniture stores, gyms, nightclubs, and theaters, effective March 28 at 5:00 p.m.[107] Montgomery enacted an indefinite, 10:00 p.m. to 5:00 a.m. curfew starting the night of March 27.[108]

On April 2, Fort Rucker issued a stay-at-home order effective until further notice.[109]

On April 3, Governor Ivey issued a statewide stay-at-home order effective April 4 until April 30.[110] Mobile issued a stay-at-home order effective April 4 until April 30.[111]

Alaska
Main article: COVID-19 pandemic in Alaska
On March 12, a cargo pilot has the first confirmed case of COVID-19 in Alaska.[112] Events of all kinds and sizes, including concerts, conventions, and sporting events, are canceled throughout the state.[113]

On March 13, schools closed through April 30 to limit the spread of the coronavirus.[114]

On March 18, Alaska government banned dine-in restaurant in an effort to stop the spread of coronavirus.[115] Anchorage medical providers have also opened drive-thru coronavirus testing in the city, but due to the shortage of test kits, referrals are needed before testing is administered.[10]

Anchorage issued a stay-at-home order.[116] Ketchikan and Sitka have issued shelter-in-place recommendations.[117][118]

On March 25, Haines Borough issued a shelter-in-place order effective until April 15. Skagway issued a shelter-in-place order effective through April 8.[119]

On March 27, Governor Mike Dunleavy issued a statewide stay-at-home mandate, ban on intrastrate travel between communities, self-quarantine requirements for out-of-state visitors, and authorization for certain small communities to impose tighter restrictions on travel, effective March 28, to be reevaluated by April 11.[120][121]

Arizona
Main article: COVID-19 pandemic in Arizona § Government Response
On March 11, a public health emergency was announced by Governor Doug Ducey.[122]

On March 12, event closures included Six Flags Hurricane Harbor Phoenix.[123] The Alhambra Elementary School District in Phoenix and other school districts throughout the state began to announce closures.[124]

On March 15, Governor Ducey and Superintendent of Public Instruction Kathy Hoffman announced the statewide closure of all Arizona schools through Friday, March 27.[125] The closure was later extended on March 30 to the end of the school year.[126]

On March 19, Governor Doug Ducey announced additional mitigation strategies to combat the spread of COVID-19 in Arizona. The measures include activating the Arizona National Guard to assist logistics. Ducey also issued executive orders to halt all elective surgeries in the state, extend expiration dates of drivers licenses 6 months, and to restrict restaurants in counties with confirmed COVID-19 cases to provide dine-out options only. The order also instructed all bars, movie theaters, and gyms to be closed.[15]

On March 30, Gov. Ducey issued a statewide stay at home order to stop the spread of new coronavirus, barring Arizonans from leaving their residences except for food, medicine and other essentials. The order was to take effect at the close of business March 31 until April 30.[127][128]

Arkansas

On March 11, Governor Asa Hutchinson declared a public health emergency in response to the first presumptive case in the state.[129]

On March 12, the governor ordered school closings in Saline, Jefferson, Pulaski, and Grant counties until March 30.[17]

On March 14, the Arkansas National Guard was activated to help assist in logistics, transportation, and in manning the call centers.[130]

On March 15, Hutchinson reported during a press conference that all public schools in the state would be closing starting on March 17.[131]

On March 17, Governor Hutchinson closed casinos for two weeks and loosened restrictions on claiming unemployment benefits for 30 days.[132]

On March 19, Hutchinson extended public school closures until April 17 and ordered restaurants and bars to offer take-out services only.[133]

On April 3, Benton and surrounding Saline County issued a stay-at-home order for juveniles effective April 6 until further notice.[134]

California
Main article: COVID-19 pandemic in California § Government response
On March 4, when 53 cases had been confirmed within the state and there had been one fatality, Governor Gavin Newsom declared a state of emergency.[135]

On March 7, all schools within the Elk Grove Unified School District in the Sacramento area shut down after a case of the virus appeared in Elk Grove.[136]

On March 9, Santa Clara County public health officer Dr. Sara Cody banned public gatherings of more than 1000 people; over the next few days she would expand the bans to cover all gatherings from Sharks games to library story hours, and to close all bars and pubs.[137]

On March 12, Governor Newsom announced that mass gatherings over 250 people were banned until the end of March.[138] He also issued an order to permit the state to commandeer hotels and medical facilities to treat coronavirus patients.[139]

On March 15, Governor Newsom called for voluntary closure of bars and in-home self-isolation of seniors 65 and older, as well as persons at-risk due to underlying conditions.[140]


A public safety alert sent on March 16 by Santa Clara County, California about the shelter-in-place order
On March 16, the public health departments of six San Francisco Bay Area counties issued shelter-in-place orders and ordered all non-essential businesses to close as of March 17.[141] It was the first such order in the mainland United States.[142]

In the following days, other cities and counties followed suit. On March 17, Ventura,[143] Monterey,[144] Sonoma,[145] San Benito,[146] and Santa Cruz counties[144] issued shelter-in-place orders.

On March 18, Mendocino,[147] Solano,[148] Lake,[149] Napa,[150] and San Luis Obispo[151] counties and Palm Springs,[152] issued shelter-in-place orders. The health officer for Yuba and Sutter counties also issued a stay-at-home order.[153]

On March 19, Governor Newsom announced a statewide stay at home order with all residents except those working in essential occupations urged to stay at home.[154][155] Governor Newsom said Wednesday that the state has asked the Department of Defense to deploy the USNS Mercy hospital ship in California.[156] The lockdown will be voluntarily enforced and police will not arrest offenders. No end date was announced.[19][157] Also, Yolo,[158] El Dorado,[159] Placer,[160] and Sacramento counties[161] and the cities of Los Angeles[162] and Fresno[163] issued shelter-in-place orders.

After the statewide order was issued, some counties continued to issue local orders to reinforce it. On March 20, Humboldt County issued a shelter-in-place order effective until further notice.[164] Amador County issued a stay-at-home order effective until April 3.[165] Inyo County issued a stay-at-home order effective until further notice.[166] Merced County issued a stay-at-home order effective until further notice.[167]

On March 21, Los Angeles County issued a safer-at-home order, initially specified to last one month.[168] The orders were modified over time but were expected to remain in place at least through July, county Public Health Director Barbara Ferrer said on May 12.[169]

On March 21, San Joaquin County also issued a stay-at-home order.[170]

On March 27, Calaveras County issued a stay-at-home order.[171]

On March 27, San Diego County issued a stay-at-home order effective March 29 until further notice.[172]

On March 31, Kings County issued a shelter-in-place order.[173] Imperial County issued a stay-at-home order effective April 1 until further notice.[174] Stanislaus County issued a stay-at-home order effective April 2.[175]

On April 4, Riverside County issued a stay-at-home and face covering order effective through April 30.[176]

On April 10, Colusa County issued a shelter-in-place order effective through May 8.[177]

On April 15, Nevada County issued a stay-at-home order effective until May 15.[178]

On March 10, Governor Polis declared a state of disaster emergency. The announced emergency rules on paid sick leave for certain industries, like food handling, hospitality and child care; unemployment insurance for those who test positive for coronavirus; work from home rules for state employees; allow people over 65 years old to renew drivers licenses online; and a new drive-up lab in east Denver. San Miguel County imposed a stay-at-home order effective March 18 through April 3 at the earliest.[183]

On March 23, the cities of Denver and Boulder issued a stay-at-home order effective March 24 through April 10 at the earliest, and Pitkin County issued a stay-at-home order effective immediately.[184]

On March 25, Boulder, Jefferson, Broomfield, Adams, Arapahoe, and Douglas counties issued stay-at-home orders effective March 26 through April 17.[185] Erie extended the order to the entire city, including portions in Weld County.[186] These jurisdictions rescinded their orders in favor of the statewide order on March 26.[187][188]

On March 25, Larimer County issued a stay-at-home order effective March 26 through April 17.[189]
